Dal Fry Full Recipe

Description

Dal fry is a popular Indian lentil dish where soft cooked lentils (dal) are simmered with a fried tempering of ghee, whole and ground spices, onions, tomatoes, and herbs

Ingredients

  • For cooking dal

  • 1 cup toor dal (also called arhar dal or split pigeon peas)
  • 3 cups water
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste

  • For Ghee Tadka

  • 2 tbsp ghee or oil
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1-2 green chilies, slit or chopped
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 large tomato, finely chopped
  • ½ tsp red chili powder
  • ½ tsp garam masala
  • ¼ tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder (optional)
  • Salt, to adjust
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)
  • 1 tsp lemon juice (optional, for tang)

Step by step instructions

  • Cook the dal

  1. Rinse 1 cup toor dal thoroughly under running water.
  2. In a pressure cooker (or a pot), add the dal, 3 cups of water, ½ tsp turmeric, and a pinch of salt.
  3. Pressure cook for 3-4 whistles (or simmer until soft and mushy if using a pot).
  4. Once done, mash the dal slightly with the back of a spoon or a whisk. Set aside.

  • Prepare the Tadka

  1. Heat 2 tbsp ghee or oil in a pan.
  2. Add 1 tsp cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  4. Add green chilies and ginger-garlic paste. Sauté until the raw smell disappears.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and the oil begins to separate.
  6. Add spices: red chili powder, turmeric, coriander powder, and garam masala. Mix well.

  • Combine Dal and Tadka and Serve

  1. Pour the cooked dal into the pan with the tadka. Mix well.
  2. Add some water to adjust the consistency as desired.
  3. Simmer for 5-7 minutes on low heat, stirring occasionally.
  4. Adjust salt and spice.
  5. Garnish with fresh coriander and a few drops of lemon juice (optional).

Serve hot with steamed rice, jeera rice, or roti.
