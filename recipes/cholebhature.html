Chole Bhature Full Recipe

Description

Chole Bhature also known as Chana Bhatura is one of the most popular Punjabi dish liked almost all over India. Chole stands for a spiced tangy chickpea curry and Bhatura is a soft and fluffy fried leavened bread. Chola Bhatura always make for a delicious and filling meal. In this recipe post, I have included the step-by-step pictorial of making both the chana curry as well as a quick bhatura that is made without yeast.

Ingredients for Chole

  • 1 cup chickpeas
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 pods badi elaichi black cardamom
  • whole laung cloves
  • 1 tea bag
  • small pinch baking soda
  • 1.25 tsp salt
  • 1 medium red onion
  • 1 medium tomato
  • 1/2 tsp garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ginger, minced
  • 4 tbsp oil
  • 3/4 tsp sukha dhaniya (dry coriander powder)
  • 3 tsp chana masala
  • 1 tsp ground anardana ground dried pomegranate powder, optional
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • dhaniya (cilantro), for garnish

Ingredients for Bhature

  • 2.5 cups maida (white all-purpose flour)
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 1 pinch baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 2 tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/4 cup water

Step by step instructions for Chole

  1. Wash and soak chickpeas for 8 hours or overnight.
  2. Add chickpeas, water, cardamom, cloves, tea bag, baking soda, and 3/4 tsp salt to a pressure cooker.
  3. Turn heat to high and allow this to cook until one whistle sounds.
  4. Once whistle sounds, turn heat down to simmer for an additional 20 minutes.
  5. Set this aside for the time being.
  6. In a separate kadai or wok, add oil and turn on the heat to high.
  7. Once oil is hot, add ginger and garlic. Stir to mix.
  8. Add in the onion. Sautee until translucent.
  9. Add tomatoes and stir to mix.
  10. Add the remaining salt, coriander powder, chana masala, and anardana.
  11. Cook until tomatoes have cooked.
  12. Drain the chickpeas but preserve the water in which it cooked.
  13. Add in the chickpeas and about 1/4 cup of the chickpea water.
  14. Cover and simmer for approximately 5 minutes so the chickpeas can absorb the flavor from the gravy.
  15. At this point you can adjust the salt and add in any additional chickpea water to get the gravy to the desired consistency.
  16. Add garam masala and stir to mix.
  17. Garnish with cilantro.

Step by step instruction for Bhature

  1. Sift together flour, baking powder, and baking soda.
  2. Add yogurt, salt, and sugar. Mix well.
  3. Add water little by little and combine ingredients into a dough.
  4. Knead the dough well, about 2-3 minutes.
  5. Rub a little oil on the dough, cover with a damp cloth, and rest for 30 minutes.
  6. Add oil to a kadai or wok to prep for deep frying. Heat on medium high.
  7. Divide the dough into 8-10 pieces and form each piece into a ball.
  8. To make a bhatura, roll out a dough ball to flatten. Get the dough as thin as you can without any tears. Add oil to the dough as needed if the dough is too sticky.
  9. Drop flattened dough into the frying oil.
  10. Once the bottom of the dough is cooked, flip the dough.
  11. Once both sides are cooked, remove and place on a paper towel to remove residual oil.
  12. Repeat with remaining bhaturas.
